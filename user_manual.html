User Guide: Jayd Hill

What is my Style?

  • (Green/INFP) I have a mediator personality. I enjoy approaching problems, thinking about the bigger picture, and connecting different types of people. I love alternate perspectives, languages, and solutions.
  • I am motivated by experiences that help me grow more into my abilities and skills.
  • I favor scenarios where my brain is active and may have trouble in envorionments that value more standard-set procedures that don't require a unique touch to complete.

What do I value?

  • I value efficiency, functionality, cleanliness, minimalism, peace, and organization.
  • I want to spend my time most effectively to create the biggest pay off.

What do I not have patience for?

  • I understand the importance of needing to find the right candidate for the job, but believe in me before you underestimate me.
  • I also value respect and look for it in all interactions. If this fundamental requirement is overlooked, I think critically about people's inner biases and am more reserved in the way I trust.
  • I also prefer going through processes myself, rather than watching other people do it. In some cases, learning is better through instruction, but creativity is best through experience. I prefer to think for myself.

What do people misunderstand about me?

  • I don’t express affection, but I am a very caring person. Your wellbeing means a lot to me. Even if we’re not close or haven’t worked together for long, I like seeing people at peace.
  • People often times see me as more extraverted because I have a bold personality. However, when it comes to expressing my preferences in projects and strategies, I can become very shy as I view these as more personal than something to be shared.
  • I prefer to think things through with myself before opening up to others. My thinking process is highly introverted.

How do I prefer people to communicate with me?

  • Start with your main concern or what you are trying to accomplish. This will give me context for the rest of the situation. I am very problem-solving oriented. I collect details, opinions, and resources so that I am properly equipped for any situation.
  • If you don’t want a problem solver and would prefer a listening ear, you have to let me know beforehand. I don’t mind listening either, I am just eager to help in any way I can.
  • I don’t do a lot of small talk. I think even the smallest actions can carry a lot of meaning, and for this reason, I don't put a lot of time in trivial or fleeting things.

How do I make decisions?

  • I make decisions very intuitively. I have set rules for myself that I am always in harmony with, and then I make decisions based on how I’m feeling within these guidelines.
  • I value the opinion of my coworkers and mentors very highly and consider their input before moving forward.
  • After I've decided on something, I follow through without hesitation.

How can people help me?

  • I prefer it when people are up front with me. I value authenticity and directness.
  • Of course criticism can be hard to take, but I believe that it should be shared anyways.
  • I respect it when people notice details about my work.
